## Three Ways to Actually Transfer Files From Google Drive to PikPak

### 1. Manual download-then-upload

The simplest, most universal method: download files from Google Drive to your device, then upload them into your PikPak drive (or via the PikPak desktop/web client). It costs nothing extra but is slow for large libraries and eats your local bandwidth and disk space along the way.

### 2. Cloud-to-cloud transfer tools

Several third-party services specialize in moving files directly between cloud accounts without routing them through your device:

- Sign into the transfer tool and connect both accounts. You select Google Drive as your source and PikPak as the destination, which directs exactly where your files will move between the two services.

- These tools typically offer filtering options to narrow down which files transfer, plus support for continuous synchronization rather than one-off jobs.

- Other tools work by logging into your Google account, granting the necessary permissions, then adding PikPak as a second connected account from the same dashboard.

This route is best if you have a large library and want a "set it and forget it" job that runs in the background.

### 3. WebDAV-based migration (for Premium users)

If you already have PikPak Premium, you can connect through WebDAV rather than a manual sync app: third-party migration tools connect to PikPak using WebDAV credentials generated from Settings → Experimental Features, and once connected, transfers run server-to-server over TLS. This tends to be the fastest and most stable option for big libraries, since it's useful specifically for migrating large libraries that would otherwise saturate your home internet for days, and you can schedule the transfer hourly, daily, or weekly.

> **Quick tip:** WebDAV access on PikPak is a Premium feature, so if you're planning a serious migration rather than moving a handful of files, it's worth activating Premium first — especially since new sign-ups can claim a free trial period through an invite code.
